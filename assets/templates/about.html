About this website

Purpose

This website hosts all of my writings and notes, or articles as called here, which I decided to share publicly. Part of it are summarizations of various books or their chapters, usually of technical nature. There are some guides and references which I made to make life and work easier. Some of the notes are just simple lists, even personal e.g.¬†my book library. There is no unifying theme - this work is mostly a product of my affinity with reading and writing down text with the goal of presenting concepts, condensing information and explaining core principles in a simpler way for some future time when knowledge might be needed. There is also joy to be found in writing itself, therefore I don‚Äôt expect all of the articles to be of technical or practical nature.

Articles & Tags

The main item served by the website is an article. I chose a general term to detach myself from commitment to any common structure. Articles are not all dedicated to a single topic, and are mostly independent; in fact, I expect that very few articles will be interlinked. There is a mechanism for grouping articles by topic: tags, which is a general name for keywords, themes or areas to which articles are dedicated. Each article has at least one tag assigned. This allows for categorization, which is only a more free form of inter-linking. The concepts of articles and tags are deeply baked in the design of the website, to the point that even the standard supporting pages like Home and About are internally stored as articles with the meta tag assigned to them.

Languages

This is a bilingual website, but not in the way in which all articles are written in two languages, but in a way where most articles are written in English and a minority is written in Serbian. If the context was more fitting for an article in Serbian, I wrote it in Serbian. Also, in cases where the article is a product of digitalization of written notes, I kept the original language, which is mostly Serbian. Articles written in Serbian are tagged with the sr-lang tag.
